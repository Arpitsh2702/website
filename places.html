India, a country with a rich cultural heritage and diverse landscapes. The best places to visit in India vary depending on the season.

Here’s a list of beautiful places in India to visit based on the best season to experience them:

Winter (November - February):
        1. Manali, Himachal Pradesh – Snow-covered mountains, adventure sports.
        2. Gulmarg, Jammu & Kashmir – Snow skiing, scenic beauty.
        3. Rann of Kutch, Gujarat – White salt desert, Rann Utsav festival.
        4. Udaipur, Rajasthan – City of lakes, palaces, and pleasant winter climate.
        5. Auli, Uttarakhand – Skiing, snowboarding, and panoramic views of the Himalayas.
Spring (March - April):
        1. Valley of Flowers, Uttarakhand – Blooming meadows of rare flowers.
        2. Ooty, Tamil Nadu – Lush greenery, blooming gardens, tea estates.
        3. Shillong, Meghalaya – Waterfalls, cherry blossoms, misty landscapes.
        4. Coorg, Karnataka – Coffee plantations, pleasant weather, vibrant nature.
        5. Darjeeling, West Bengal – Tea gardens, scenic train rides, and clear views of Kanchenjunga.
Summer (May - June):
        1. Leh-Ladakh – High-altitude adventure, scenic monasteries, and clear skies.
        2. Spiti Valley, Himachal Pradesh – Rugged landscapes, monasteries, and treks.
        3. Nainital, Uttarakhand – Cool climate, lakes, and mountain views.
        4. Munnar, Kerala – Tea gardens, cool hills, waterfalls.
        5. Tawang, Arunachal Pradesh – Buddhist monasteries, snow-capped peaks, and peaceful landscapes.
Monsoon (July - September):
        1. Munnar, Kerala – Lush green hills, tea plantations, monsoon mist.
        2. Meghalaya – Cherrapunji, waterfalls, living root bridges.
        3. Goa – Green countryside, monsoon festivals, quiet beaches.
        4. Udaipur, Rajasthan – Palaces and lakes, especially scenic during rains.
        5. Coorg, Karnataka – Misty weather, coffee estates, waterfalls.
Autumn (October - November):
        1. Jodhpur, Rajasthan – Forts, palaces, and cultural festivals.
        2. Rishikesh, Uttarakhand – River rafting, yoga retreats, pleasant climate.
        3. Hampi, Karnataka – Historical ruins, UNESCO World Heritage site.
        4. Pushkar, Rajasthan – Camel fair, desert landscapes, and cultural experiences.
        5. Sundarbans, West Bengal – Mangrove forests, wildlife, particularly the Bengal tiger.
